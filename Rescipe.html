GulabJamun

Ingredient

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • ¼ cup all purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • 3 tbsp ghee
  • ¼ cup + 3 tbsp milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • Juice of half lemon
  • ½ tsp rose water
  • 1 tsp cardamom powder
  • ¼ tsp saffron
  • Oil/ghee to fry

Recipe

  1. Take the milk, all pupose flour, baking powder and ghee in a bowl
  2. Mix well using your fingers
  3. Pour milk and mix into stiff dough
  4. Close the lid and keep aside for 15 minutes
  5. Prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, lemon juice, rose water, cardamom powder and saffron in a thick bottomed vessel
  6. Boil the syrup for at least 7 minutes
  7. When the syrup boils rapidly, turn off the flame and close the lid
  8. Make sure that the syrup stays hot
  9. make 20 small balls from the dough
  10. If the dough is too stiff, add some milk to soften it
  11. Heat an unniyappam pan and add enough ghee into the holes
  12. Lower the flame when the ghee is hot
  13. Add the jamun balls into each hole
  14. Fey untill the jamun balls are brown on all sides
  15. Transfer the fried jamun into the sugae syrup while they are hot
  16. Strir the syrup gently without breaking the jamun
  17. Close the lid and keep aside for 15 minutes
  18. Soft and plump gulab jamun are ready!

Barfi

Ingredient

  • 2 cups (250 g) whole raw cashew nuts
  • 2 tablespoons (15 g) whole milk powder
  • ½ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 10 tablespoons plus 1 ½ teaspoons (125 g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (60 ml) water
  • Rounded ½ teaspoon ghee
  • Edible silver leaf or vark, optional

Recipe

  1. In a blender, grind cashews to a fine powder, taking care not to grind them so thoroughly that the nuts release their

    oil. Place cashew powder in a medium mixing bowl, then whisk in milk powder and cardamom.

  2. In a 2-quart saucepan, heat sugar and water over medium-high heat until sugar has melted.

    Continue cooking until the mixture reaches 230°F (110°C) on a probe or candy thermometer,

    about 4 minutes. Alternatively, you can judge the doneness of the candy syrup by placing

    a drop of it on your thumb, making sure to let it cool enough first that you don't burn

    yourself. Allow the drop of syrup to cool to room temperature, then pinch it between your

    thumb and index finger and pull them apart; the syrup is done when a single strand stretches

    between your fingers without breaking.

  3. Turn heat to low and add cashew mixture, stirring continuously with a heat-resistant flexible spatula.

    The mixture should pull away from the sides of the pan and form a loose ball. Add ghee and mix well

    for a few seconds, then remove pan from heat.

  4. Place a sheet of parchment paper on a clean work surface and carefully scoop hot cashew dough on top.

    Set a second piece of parchment paper on top of the cashew dough. Knead the hot cashew dough through

    the paper for a few seconds to help bring it together. Using a rolling pin, roll cashew dough out

    into a single even sheet about 1/8 inch (3mm) thick. Remove top layer of parchment.

  5. Let dough stand 1 minute. If desired, carefully lay edible silver leaf (silver vark) all over the dough.

    Then, using a sharp knife, cut dough into diamond-shaped pieces 1 to 1 1/2 inches (2.5 to 4cm) long.

    Allow to rest at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.
